The gap

You know the feeling

You see an incredible build at Cars and Coffee or SEMA. A lifted 4Runner with a full rack, light bar, and skid plates you've never seen before. You want to know what A/T tires are those? What suspension? What bumper? What light bar is throwing that amber pattern?

So you do what everyone has historically done. You wait in line to talk to the owner, or walk up and attempt to ask. But the owner's not around, or you can't wait 20 min in line. You take a photo, try to find a social handle, and attempt to DM them on IG or FBK. Maybe they respond in three days. Maybe never. You drop your question on a pic, scroll through the comment sections hoping someone asked your question prior, optimistic the maybe answer is still accurate. Or maybe you ask in a Facebook group or on a YTB video.

Days later you still don't have a straight answer from the owner.

Now what? Take a risk and potentially buy the wrong part? Get frustrated by the number of options, and give up?

The builds exist. The Go-To place for information doesn't. That's the gap BuildBio fills.

The philosophy

Every build matters

This is not a platform for exotics only. The $2,000 daily with a cold air intake and aftermarket wheels matters. The 200,000-mile Tacoma with a budget lift and LED pods matters. The rust bucket project car in the driveway that's been "almost done" for three years — that matters too.

If you turned a wrench on it, if you chose a part, if you made it yours — it belongs here. Budget builds. Dream builds. Daily drivers. Weekend warriors. Trail rigs. Show cars. Every single one.

Where we're headed

The largest library of documented builds

BuildBio isn't just a social media app, it is a permanent record. Every build documented here adds to a growing library of real, verified modifications — the kind of resource the car world has never actually had.

Owners* can earn revenue when their documented builds drive affiliate sales. The community gets trustworthy, verified data. The industry gets real insight into what people are actually installing on real vehicles. Everyone wins.

The vision is simple: every modified vehicle on the road should have a BuildBio profile. The more builds that get documented, the more useful the platform becomes for everyone — and the owners who contribute, and purposefully steer visitors to their profile, can make some money at the same time.
